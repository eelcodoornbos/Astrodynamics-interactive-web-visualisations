Fundamentals of Astrodynamics

Velocity components

When the flight path angle, $\gamma$, is introduced, the radial and normal velocity components and the angular momentum, per unit of mass, of the motion can be expressed as: $$V_r = {\color{red}\dot{r}} = {\color{velocity}V} \sin \gamma$$ $$V_\theta = {\color{blue}r\dot{\theta}} = {\color{velocity}V} \cos \gamma$$ $$H = r {\color{velocity}V} \cos \gamma$$ Differentation of (5.22) to time yields another expression for the radial velocity component: $${\color{red}\dot{r}} = \frac{(H^2/\mu)e\dot\theta\sin\theta}{(1+e\cos\theta)^2} = r^2\dot\theta \frac{\mu e}{H^2}\sin\theta = \frac{\mu}{H}e \sin\theta$$ An expression for the normal velocity component follows directly from (5.7) and (5.22): $${\color{blue}r\dot{\theta}} = \frac{H}{r} = \frac{\mu}{H} ( 1 + e \cos \theta )$$ From (5.23), (5.24), (5.26) and (5.27) we obtain for the flight path angle: $$\tan \gamma = \frac{\dot{r}}{r\dot{\theta}} = \frac{e \sin\theta}{1+e\cos\theta}$$
